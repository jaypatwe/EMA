The Problem: Rethinking Vehicle Insurance Claims Processing
Your job is to design a next-generation Claims Processing product for vehicle insurance companies that leverages agentic AI benefits.
Background: The Current State of Claims Processing
Today's claims management systems are complex systems of record designed to manage the end-to-end lifecycle of a claim, from the initial report to the final payment. They are often legacy systems, heavily customized, and require significant manual intervention from claims handlers.
Here's a brief overview of this problem space:
Key Stakeholders:
Claimant / Policyholder: The customer reporting the accident.
Claims Intake Specialist: The call center or web agent who receives the initial report (FNOL).
Claims Adjuster: The primary user who investigates the claim, assesses damage, determines liability, and authorizes a settlement.
Appraiser / Estimator: (Internal or 3rd party) Assesses the vehicle damage and estimates repair costs.
SIU (Special Investigations Unit): The internal team that investigates potential fraud.
Claims Manager: Oversees adjuster workloads, KPIs, and customer satisfaction.
High-Level Processes:
First Notice of Loss (FNOL)
Triage & Assignment 
Investigation & Adjudication
Settlement & Payout
